# Surfaces

Source: Seed UX and Microcopy Best Practices.

Per-surface tone, length, and structure guidance. Use alongside `tov.md` (for pillar mix by funnel stage) and `microcopy.md` (for string-level patterns).

---

## Site-wide guiding principles

Apply everywhere unless a specific surface rule overrides.

1. **Write at an 8th-grade reading level.** An 8th grader and a parent in their 50s should both understand what Seed is, what makes it unique, and the benefit offered.
2. **Short, scannable sections.** Headlines 5-8 words. Supporting copy 1-2 brief sentences that do not wrap beyond two lines on desktop.
3. **Progressive disclosure.** Present information in layers. The most essential information lives on the main framework. Details live in flyovers, drawers, or linked pages. Skimming the page should still convey the core offering.
4. **Guide toward action.** Language should prompt the user to act without being transactional or pushy. Help users move forward, whether that is exploring a product or adding to cart.
5. **Focus of funnel stage shapes depth.** Homepage and PLP communicate what each product is, does, and its overarching benefit. Save mechanics for PDPs and science pages, except when explicitly featured (like the ViaCap® module on homepage).

## Homepage

**Goal:** The front door. Often the first immersive experience with Seed. Hook with intrigue, anchor in science, make the copy memorable.

**Tone mix:** Inviting, thumb-stopping, intriguing. Intrigue leads. Grounding supports.

### Hero headline
- Capture attention with one short, intriguing line that tees up the module.
- **Desktop:** 8-10 words.
- **Mobile:** 5-7 words.
- **Sentence case** with punctuation for both.
- If the hero is a bestseller (DS-01®), lean into reputation and benefits.
- If the hero introduces a new product or bundle, explain what it is and why it advances the space.

### Hero subhead
- One sentence or phrase adding key detail or nuance.
- **Desktop + mobile:** 10-12 words. No more than 2 lines.

### Performance benchmarks
- **Scroll depth:** 75%+ of users scrolling past the hero.
- **Comprehension:** value prop understood in 5 seconds (slightly longer for multi-product heroes).
- **CTR:** 15-25% click the primary CTA.
- **Bounce:** <45% drop-off after the homepage only.

### Industry benchmarks for reference
- Oura's homepage hero achieves ~80% scroll depth by leading with a specific measurable benefit.
- Function Health sees 22% CTA clicks on benefit-driven heroes vs. 14% on feature-driven.

### Message testing territories (for agents generating variants)
- **Benefits-led vs. feature-led:** `The probiotic for daily relief and gut support` vs. `The clinically proven daily probiotic + prebiotic`.
- **Branded vs. direct response:** `The future of your health is microbial` vs. `To improve your daily health, start with your microbiome`.
- **Direct product name vs. indirect:** `Meet DM-02™, the daily multivitamin for you + your microbiome` vs. `Meet the daily multivitamin designed for you + your microbiome.`
- **Module order:** Co-biotics hero first then DS-01 spotlight vs. DS-01 spotlight first then Co-Biotics.

## PLP (Product Listing Page)

**Goal:** Orient the user to the full product suite. Tie products together through Seed's approach (the microbiome). Route to the right PDP.

**Tone mix:** Credible, confident, human. Grounding-dominant with illumination.

### PLP headline
- 5-10 words.
- Reflect the scope of the suite and what ties products together (the microbiome).
- Subject to change as new SKUs ship.
- **Sentence case**, include punctuation.

### PLP subhead (optional)
- Use when the suite expands or requires more explanation (e.g., products beyond the microbiome).

### Section headers
Use the full product or bundle name: "DS-01® Daily Synbiotic" or "Daily Essentials Duo."

### Product descriptions
- Use standardized descriptions approved by Copy, PMM, and Growth.
- **Desktop:** single phrase that leads with the product benefit and functional outcome.
- **Mobile:** shorter phrase that clearly communicates the most important benefit or RTB.

### Example: PLP product description pattern
| Field | Example |
|---|---|
| Section Header | AM-02™ Focus + Energy |
| Desktop Description | `Delivers steady, caffeine-free energy to enhance focus, sharpen attention, and help fight mental fatigue.*` |
| Mobile Description | `Sharpen attention, fight fatigue.*` |

### PLP message tests
- **Mobile cutdowns:** full 1:1 mobile description vs. shortened mobile-optimized.
- **Promo prevalence:** simple hero banner vs. multi-placement (hero + badges + 10-second popup).

## PDP (Product Detail Page)

**Goal:** Educate on the product, answer buyer decision questions (BDQs), drive conversion. Translate scientific rigor into approachable language. New audiences and established supplement users both need to feel compelled.

**Tone mix:** Credible, confident, human. Balanced across all three pillars.

### Priority hierarchy (by scroll depth)
Assume 20-30% user drop-off per module. Front-load what matters to purchase.
- **High priority (top of page):** product function, primary benefits, key differentiators.
- **Low priority (bottom):** social proof, ingredient deep dives, mechanistic explanations.

### Industry benchmarks for reference
- Oura places social proof at 35% scroll depth and sees 18% higher engagement than at 70%.
- Grüns restructured PDP content and increased time-on-page by 41 seconds with 15% conversion lift.

### Hero headline
- **Full product name** without punctuation: `DM-02™ Daily Multivitamin`.
- If design allows, use the alphanumeric (DM-02™) in a pill or bug.

### Hero subcopy
- Single sentence, key benefits, 15 words max, include punctuation.

### Hero benefits + ingredient callouts
- One-line macro callouts on ingredient or benefit. **No periods or terminal punctuation.**
- **Desktop:** 7-8 words, avoid wrapping to 2 lines.
- **Mobile:** 5-7 words, avoid wrapping to 2 lines.

### De-risk copy (above fold, near CTA)
Short scannable phrases. Use periods.
- Answer BDQs: pricing, shipping, delivery cadence.
- Order by importance: pricing / refund first, social proof last.

### Industry benchmarks for de-risk
- AG1's "60-day money-back guarantee" above fold correlates with 19% lower cart abandonment vs. category.
- Feals' "Cancel anytime" near CTA reduced subscription-anxiety drop-off by 27%.

### Module headlines
- 8-10 words. Vary length down the page to avoid visual density.
- Lead with benefit / key feature. Active voice. Verb-led.

### Module body copy
- 2-3 sentences per section or mini-paragraph.
- Subhead carries the key point. Subcopy adds the why.
- Vary sentence length. Use bullets where scannability wins.

### Module section subheads
- 3-5 words. Function as a lamp post for a benefit, ingredient, or test.
- Can serve as card headers or secondary module titles.

### Module section subcopy
- Single sentence or phrase. Adds context or depth to the section subhead.

### Module anatomy (standard PDP structure)
1. **Hero:** product title, one-sentence description, 3-5 benefits, ingredients callout.
2. **Module 2:** headline summarizing product function and health impact. 2-4 subheads, one sentence/phrase each.
3. **Module 3 (ViaCap):** only for products using the delivery system. Headline + subhead + inner and outer capsule detail.
4. **Module 4 (Benefits Timeline):** headline + subcopy on consistency. 3-4 milestones with subcopy, PMM-approved.
5. **Module 5 (Ingredients / Formulation):** headline + subcopy on premium or novel ingredients. Cards for each ingredient or grouping.
6. **Module 6 (Comparison Chart):** PMM-provided comp charts.
7. **Module 7 (Safety Testing):** standard boilerplate headline + subhead; boilerplate subheads and copy for 3 core testing features.

### Drop-downs and drawers
Use to nest technical detail for detail-seekers:
- Ingredients or strains
- Clinical studies
- Individualized or extended product benefits
- Safety and testing

### PDP performance benchmarks
- **Add to cart rate:** 12-16%.
- **Time on page:** 2.5-4 minutes. (Biotics need more education. Industry avg: 3:45 for biotics vs. 2:30 for multivitamins.)
- **Scroll to bottom:** 30-40%.
- **Primary CTA CTR:** 15-25% in first 3-4 modules.
- **Subscription attach rate:** 60-75%.

### PDP message tests
- **Headline length:** <5 words (`2-1 formulation, superior delivery`) vs. <10 words (`Dual formulation, engineered for delivery to the gut`).
- **Benefit format:** bulleted list vs. 3-4 words with icons.
- **Module order:** benefits-early vs. benefits-late.

### PDP headline + subhead examples (approved)

**Product-centric (DM-02™)**
| Level | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | `38 trillion reasons your current multivitamin falls short.` | `Your body and your microbiome need support.` |
| Subhead | `DM-02™ nourishes your body and your microbiome—the system most supplements ignore.` | `DM-02™ is the multivitamin that delivers nutrients to both.` |
| Headline | `Going where other multivitamins simply can't.` | `The multivitamin made for a healthy microbiome.` |
| Hero Subhead | `DM-02™ delivers nutrients where they're needed most: your microbiome.` | `DM-02™ delivers support for more than just your body.` |

**Benefit-specific (PM-02™)**
| Level | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | `Deep sleep isn't a luxury—it's biology.` | `Your microbiome makes better sleep possible.` |
| Subhead | `PM-02™ targets your microbiome for deep sleep and recovery overnight.` | `PM™ helps restore your natural sleep cycle through targeted body + microbiome support.` |

**Category-level (Co-Biotics)**
| Level | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| Headline | `Your microbiome influences everything.` | `Advanced formulations for whole body health.` |
| Subhead | `Support it with advanced formulations for sleep, energy, nutrition, gut health, and more.` | `Support your body + microbiome with probiotic and co-biotic formulations.` |

## Cart and checkout

**Goal:** Simple, clear, directional copy that drives completion. Answer cart-abandonment questions before they're asked.

**Tone mix:** Reassuring, trust-building, direct. Grounding leads. Illumination answers BDQs. Intrigue is rare.

### Principles
- **Lay out all key costs upfront.** Subtotal, tax, shipping (if outside US).
- **De-risk signals.** Repeat: "adjust or cancel anytime."
- **Trust and reassurance on payment.** Surface cybersecurity signals: secure checkout badges, encryption level, security partnerships.
- **Realistic delivery estimates.** Use the carrier's range. Specify delivery time, not ship time or processing time.

### Industry benchmarks for reference
- Sakara's guest checkout option increased completion by 18%.
- Feals' exit-intent offer recovers 11% of abandonment.

## Promos, offers, and badges

For native UI elements that label a product as new, limited, or feature a special callout:
- **Under 4 words** when possible.
- **Title Case**, no punctuation.
- Exception: capitalize titles or proper nouns within the label.

### Examples
| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Promos | `40% Off`, `Save 30%` |
| Badges / Bugs | `Bestseller`, `Meet Co-Biotics`, `For Kids` |

## Email

**Tone by surface** (from the Style Guide):
- **Banner:** Title Case
- **Headline:** Title Case
- **Body:** Sentence case
- **CTA:** Title Case

### Subject lines
- Contractions allowed.
- Arrows allowed to replace emoji for urgency: `30% off → ends today.`
- Dots to separate value and timing: `30% Off • Ends Today`.
- One exclamation max, reserved for the right moment.

## Ads

**Tone by element:**
- **Headline:** Title Case
- **Body:** Sentence case
- **CTA:** Title Case

For prospecting channels (Meta, Google, TV), use familiar ingredient names (ashwagandha, ginseng root) not trademarked names.

## Social

**Tone by element:**
- **On graphic:** Title Case for headlines and fragments. Sentence case for complete sentences.
- **Body:** Sentence case
- **Captions:** Sentence case

Contractions allowed in captions and subject lines. Emojis allowed from the approved set (see `mechanics.md`).

**Supers:** Sentence case.

Contractions allowed in VO.
