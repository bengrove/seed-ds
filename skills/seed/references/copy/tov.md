# Tone of Voice

Source: Seed Brand TOV 2025 (Figma deck F0GLIVKZ0uByZmaivVhcoJ) + Seed Copy Style Guide 2026.

This file defines how Seed sounds. Read before generating any user-facing copy. When uncertain, pick the option that makes the reader feel smarter, not sold to.

---

## The three pillars

Every piece of Seed copy should carry all three. When one is missing, the tone breaks in predictable ways (see "Formulas" below).

### 1. Grounding

Speak in a human way. Keep words clear and concise. Be the trusted scientist, not the life coach. Confident, not casual.

**In practice**
- Short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Specific nouns and active verbs over adjectives.
- Factual claims with a citation or a clear source.
- Address the reader as a peer. Assume intelligence.

**Avoid**
- Jargon without definition on first use.
- Hedging language ("kind of," "sort of," "we believe").
- Corporate softeners ("we're excited to," "we're thrilled").
- Wellness platitudes ("listen to your body," "honor your journey").

### 2. Illuminating

Translate information into understanding. Use progressive disclosure. Build bridges with analogy when the science is abstract.

**In practice**
- Lead with the outcome. Follow with the mechanism.
- Define terms on first use. Link to the primary source when it helps.
- Use analogy to carry one step of complexity, not the whole idea.
- Layer detail. Headline carries the point. Body adds the why. Drawer holds the deep dive.

**Avoid**
- Stacking three analogies. Pick one.
- Jargon walls that gate understanding behind terminology.
- Pretending a hard thing is easy. Make it clear, not cute.

### 3. Intriguing

Spark curiosity. Tilt the frame. Say something unconventional, but intentionally so. Reward the reader for paying attention.

**In practice**
- Open with a counterintuitive fact, a reframe, or a specific number.
- Use rhythm. Short sentence. Longer one. Short again.
- Trust the reader to get the joke or the reference.
- Leave a seam for the reader to lean in ("…and here's why that matters").

**Avoid**
- Clickbait ("you'll never guess"). Intrigue through specificity, not withholding.
- Sensationalism. One claim per line, all of them defensible.
- Forced wordplay. If it isn't funnier than the direct line, cut it.
- Mystery for mystery's sake. There should always be a payoff.

## Formulas: what breaks when a pillar is missing

Use these as diagnostic checks when copy feels off.

### Grounding + Illuminating, no Intriguing = **Flat**

Technically correct. Educationally useful. Forgettable. No reason to keep reading.

> Flat: "DM-02 is a multivitamin with 20 essential nutrients. It is designed to support your body and microbiome."

Fix: add specificity, a reframe, or rhythm.

> Seed: "38 trillion reasons your current multivitamin falls short."

### Grounding + Intriguing, no Illuminating = **Pretentious**

Sounds smart. Feels smug. Reader doesn't learn anything. They sense the distance.

> Pretentious: "The microbial architecture of human physiology demands a precision modality."

Fix: translate. Lead with what the reader gets.

> Seed: "Your body runs on a microbiome. DM-02 feeds it."

### Illuminating + Intriguing, no Grounding = **Woo woo**

Evocative but untethered. No evidence, no source, no claim you could defend.

> Woo woo: "Reconnect with the ancient wisdom of your gut. Your body already knows."

Fix: cite, or name the mechanism.

> Seed: "The vagus nerve carries signals from gut to brain. A published 2023 study showed…"

### Grounding + Illuminating + Intriguing = **Seed**

Factual. Clear. Worth reading. The reader leaves smarter than they arrived.

> "You are not separate from Earth—you're in symbiosis with it."

## Voice distribution by funnel stage

Every piece of copy uses all three pillars, but the mix shifts by where the reader is in the journey.

### Awareness (top of funnel)
**Channels:** Meta, Google, TV, OOH, social, homepage hero, editorial partnerships.
**Mix:** Intriguing leads. Grounding supports. Illuminating is light.
**Goal:** Stop the scroll. Earn the click. Plant one idea the reader carries forward.

### Consideration (mid funnel)
**Channels:** Homepage scroll, PLPs, Cultured articles, email education flows.
**Mix:** Balanced. Grounding and Illuminating carry the page. Intriguing opens modules.
**Goal:** Move the reader from "Seed exists" to "Seed solves my specific question."

### Conversion (bottom of funnel)
**Channels:** PDPs, cart, checkout, promo banners, SMS.
**Mix:** Grounding leads. Illuminating answers buyer decision questions (BDQs). Intriguing is rare and earned.
**Goal:** Remove friction. Answer the last question. Confirm the decision.

### Retention (post-purchase)
**Channels:** Member account, onboarding, education sequences, pause/cancel flows, win-back.
**Mix:** Grounding is dominant. Illuminating deepens. Intriguing only when there is genuine news.
**Goal:** Reinforce the decision. Build trust through consistency. Treat control (pause, cancel) as a first-class feature.

## Voice rules for agents

When generating copy:
1. Check this file and `mechanics.md` first. If a rule exists, follow it.
2. If a microcopy string exists in `microcopy.md`, use it verbatim.
3. Run the formula check: if your copy only hits two pillars, it will feel flat, pretentious, or woo. Rewrite.
4. Flag any health or efficacy claim for legal review in your response. See `compliance.md`.
5. Default to the shortest copy that carries meaning. Brevity is a voice choice, not a constraint.

## Voice pillar checklist

Before you ship a headline, subhead, or module, check that it answers three questions:
- **Grounding:** Is this specific and defensible? Would a scientist nod?
- **Illuminating:** Would a smart person who is new to Seed leave understanding more?
- **Intriguing:** Is there a reason to read the next line?

If any answer is "no," rewrite before passing it to QA.
