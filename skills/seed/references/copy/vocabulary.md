Source: Seed Copy Style Guide 2026 + Seed UX and Microcopy Best Practices.

Word choices, defined terms, product naming, and Elevated vs. Simple translations. Use this file whenever you are selecting between two phrasings of the same concept.

## Defined terms

Keep these distinctions precise. They show up often and matter scientifically.

### Bacteria vs. bacterium
- **Bacteria** = plural. `Bacteria are single-cell microorganisms.`
- **Bacterium** = singular. `This bacterium is responsible for the infection.`
- Conjugate verbs accordingly.

### Species
Both singular and plural. Can be a collective noun.
- Singular use: `This species of bacteria is commonly found in the oral microbiome.`
- Plural use: `These species are linked to digestive function.`

### Strain vs. species
- A **species** is a group of bacteria with shared characteristics.
- A **strain** is a subset of a species that shares additional genetic traits.
- Seed copy emphasizes **strain specificity**. When a study references a species, say so. When a product contains a specific strain, name it (e.g., *Bifidobacterium breve* SD-BR3-IT).

### Microbiome vs. microbiota
- **Microbiota** = the microorganisms themselves.
- **Microbiome** = the microorganisms plus their genetic material and interactions with the host.
- Use **microbiome** for the ecology as a whole. Use **microbiota** for the microbes within a specific ecology.

## Brand voice: "We" vs. "Seed"

Default voice is collective ("we"). Use "Seed" when clarity, attribution, or formality is required.

### Use "we"
When speaking about what Seed does, believes, or builds.
- `We exist to bridge the gap between science and everyday life.`
- `We design for sustainability from the start.`

### Use "Seed"
- Scientific or press contexts: `Published by Seed Health.`
- Legal or compliance: `Seed is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease.`
- Formal statements: `Seed products are held to a rigorous testing standard.`
- Differentiating brand from audience: `Seed members receive early access.`

### Think of it this way
- **We** = voice, ethos, relationship with the reader.
- **Seed** = entity, company, formal name.

## Product naming

### Full names
Use the full name on first reference, then shorthand is fine.

| Alphanumeric | Full name |
|---|---|
| DS-01® | DS-01® Daily Synbiotic |
| DM-02™ | DM-02™ Daily Multivitamin |
| AM-02™ | AM-02™ Energy + Focus |
| PM-02™ | PM-02™ Sleep + Restore |
| VS-01™ | VS-01™ Vaginal Synbiotic |
| PDS-08™ | PDS-08™ Pediatric Daily Synbiotic |

### Synbiotic vs. probiotic
In acquisition placements (ads, acquisition emails), refer to DS-01® as:
- `probiotic + prebiotic`
- `2-in-1`

Use "synbiotic" everywhere else. Acquisition copy uses more familiar terms for new consumers.

### Co-Biotics: top-line descriptors

When space does not allow the full product name, use these one-liners:

| Product | Descriptor |
|---|---|
| DS-01® | Digestive and whole body health support.* |
| DM-02™ Daily Multivitamin | Daily nutrition support.* |
| AM-02™ Energy + Focus | Daily energy support.* |
| PM-02™ Sleep + Restore | Nightly sleep support.* |
| VS-01™ Vaginal Synbiotic | Vaginal microbiome support.* |
| PDS-08® Pediatric Synbiotic | Digestive and whole body health for kids 3-18.* |

### Articles ahead of product names
Drop "the" before DS-01® or PDS-08™, the way you drop "the" before iPhone.

OK to say:
- `Our DS-01® / PDS-08™`
- `Your DS-01® / PDS-08™`
- `the DS-01® / PDS-08™ formulation` (product name used as an adjective)

## Trademarks

Always superscript. Use in these cases; don't drop once you've used the full name earlier in the same piece.

### ™ Trademarks
DM-02™ Daily Multivitamin, AM-02™ Energy + Focus, PM-02™ Sleep + Restore, VS-01™ Vaginal Synbiotic, PDS-08™ Pediatric Daily Synbiotic, BacTrac™, BioPatty™.

### ® Trademarks
DS-01® Daily Synbiotic, ViaCap®, CereBoost® Ginseng Root, TeaCrine® Theacrine.

### Ingredient trademarks: context rules
- **Prospecting (Meta / Google / TV):** familiar ingredient names. `ashwagandha, nootropics derived from ginseng root, theacrine`.
- **Owned and retail PDPs:** both familiar and trademarked names where space allows. When tight, default to familiar.

### Trademarks + disclaimers
Punctuation can stack in tight headlines or banners. Rearrange the line or use the full product name to avoid visual clutter.

## Category naming

### Biotics (category)
Microbiome solutions for systemic health.

**Subcategory 01: Synbiotics.** Prebiotic + probiotic supplements.

**Subcategory 02: Co-Biotics.** Nutritional compounds for you + your microbiome.

### Descriptions are grounded in
1. Formula foundation.
2. Functional benefit.
3. Consumer market landscape.

Descriptions **may** include category differentiators of "Biotics."

### Naming criteria
- Indicate what the product **is** and **does**.
- Intuitive in new distribution channels.
- Grounded in sense-making.
- Does not dilute the science. Underscores efficacy.
- Fits current product suite and naming conventions.

## Elevated vs. Simple scientific language

Seed operates two registers. Pick based on audience.

- **Elevated / Scientific:** PDP hero copy, editorial, PMM-approved surfaces, members familiar with the category.
- **Simple Scientific:** acquisition surfaces, top-of-funnel marketing, audiences new to Seed.

### General

| Elevated / Scientific | Simple Scientific |
|---|---|
| Gut microbiome | Gut health |
| Colon | GI tract / GI system |
| Gut-brain axis | Nervous system / vagus nerve |
| Screened as a full formula | All ingredients within the product are screened |
| Targeted nutrient delivery | Engineered to make it to the gut |
| Allergens, pesticides, and contaminants | Heavy metals, glyphosate, gluten, dairy, and soy |
| Essential nutrients | Vitamins and minerals |
| Co-Biotics | Prebiotics, postbiotics, and nutrients |

### Sleep (PM-02™)

| Elevated | Simple |
|---|---|
| Next-day haze | Grogginess / fogginess |
| Sleep onset / latency | Fall asleep faster |
| Sleep-wake cycle | Circadian rhythm |
| Bioidentical melatonin | Melatonin that mimics the body's natural levels |

### Focus and energy (AM-02™)

| Elevated | Simple |
|---|---|
| Mental clarity / sharpness | Brain fog |
| Nootropics | Plant-based support |
| Cognitive support | Supports clear thinking |
| Energy support for the body | Fast-acting energy |

### Nutrition (DM-02™)

| Elevated | Simple |
|---|---|
| Bioavailable nutrients | Easy to absorb nutrients |
| 2-in-1 formulation | 2 formulas in one capsule |
| Replenishes your body and microbiome | Feeds / nourishes the body and microbiome |

### Rule for agents
If the target surface is ambiguous, default to **Simple Scientific** and flag the choice in your response for review.

## Do / Don't phrasing patterns

From the Style Guide's approved-copy table. Use these as direct reference when composing similar lines.

### Promotional framing

| Do | Not this |
|---|---|
| `DS-01® Now 25% Off†` | `25% Off DS-01®†` |

Lead with the product, not the discount. Discount is the hook, not the identity.

### Product benefit framing

| Do | Not this |
|---|---|
| `Daily DS-01® seeds your gut with beneficial bacteria.` | `When you take DS-01®, you are seeding your gut with beneficial bacteria.` |

Active voice. Product as subject.

### Claim-based headlines

| Do | Not this |
|---|---|
| `Relieve Bloating in 1 Week*` | `Relieve Bloating in 1 Week With DS-01®*` |

Lead with the outcome. Attach the product in the subhead or supporting copy.

## Words to avoid in marketing copy

### Corporate softeners
`We're excited to`, `We're thrilled`, `We're proud to announce`.

### Wellness platitudes
`Listen to your body`, `Honor your journey`, `Your best self`, `Reset and recharge`, `Glow from within`.

### Vague benefit claims
`Boosts`, `supercharges`, `unlocks`, `activates`. Replace with the specific outcome or mechanism.

### Sensational CTAs
`Act fast!`, `Don't miss out!`, `Last chance!`. Use neutral urgency: `30% off • Ends today`.

### Condescending educational tone
`Did you know…`, `Here's a fun fact…`, `You might be surprised to learn…`.

### Fear-based framing
`Without Seed, your gut…`, `Most multivitamins fail you…`. State what Seed does, not what competitors lack (unless you can cite the comparison).

## Words to prefer

### Outcomes over features
- `Supports healthy digestion` > `Contains 24 strains`
- Then follow with the feature for the detail-seeker.

### Mechanism over mystery
- `The vagus nerve carries signals from gut to brain` > `Your gut talks to your brain`

### Specificity over adjectives
- `24 clinically studied strains` > `advanced strains`
- `4-6 weeks to notice a difference` > `results fast`
