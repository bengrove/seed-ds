# Mechanics

Source: Seed Copy Style Guide 2026 (BRN_Seed_Copy_Style_Guide_2026.docx).

Grammar, punctuation, formatting, and typography rules. When a rule here conflicts with a general style guide (AP, APA), this file wins for Seed work.

---

## Punctuation

### Oxford commas
Always. Seed is a pro-Oxford comma company.
- Do: "probiotics, prebiotics, and synbiotics"
- Not: "probiotics, prebiotics and synbiotics"

### Em dashes
Preferred for breaking up longer sentences. Use instead of semicolons.
- **No spaces** on either end: `ecosystems—they contain`
- Not: `ecosystems — they contain`
- Do not confuse with en dashes (number ranges: "ages 3–17") or hyphens (compound words).

### Exclamation points
Rarely. One per surface max. A sharper rewrite carries more impact than a bang.
- Allowed: community, social engagement, celebration moments.
- Avoid: product copy, promotional urgency ("Act fast!"), headlines.

### Quotation marks
- **Headers:** single quotes. `Goop says DS-01® is 'the absolute best'`
- **Body:** double quotes. `Goop stated DS-01® is "the absolute best probiotic."`

### Dots (promo separators)
Use a dot with a space on each side to separate value and timing in promo banners and subject lines. Don't use pipes or slashes.
- Do: `30% Off • Ends Today`
- Not: `30% Off | Ends Today`

### Arrows
Can replace emojis for promo urgency in subject lines or preheaders.
- Example: `30% off → ends today.`

### Hyphens
- Use for compound adjectives: "capsule-in-capsule technology," "6-month supply," "best-selling synbiotic."
- Exception: do not hyphenate -ly adverbs. Write "clinically studied" not "clinically-studied."
- For product names in plain text, use a standard hyphen (DM-02™). In design pills, use an en dash.

### Spacing
Do a final pass. No extra spaces around em dashes, before/after emojis, or at paragraph starts.

## Citations and references

### Style
APA for everything except the Strains Mapped to Studies chart on `/reference` pages (NLM, for compliance).

### In-text placement
Citations come **after** punctuation, except with em dashes.
- Do: `Science is cool.¹`
- Not: `Science is cool¹.`
- Em dash exception: `Science is cool¹—especially biology.`

### Citation list formatting
Put a space between the superscript and the reference text.
- Do: `1 Indoor Air Quality. (2021, September 7). US EPA.`
- Not: `1Indoor Air Quality. (2021, September 7). US EPA.`

### Source
APA generator: bibme.org (slow, ad-heavy, but free).

## Footnote marks (non-citation)

Use distinct symbols for distinct purposes. Always superscript. Place after the first instance of the callout, except in email subject lines or preheaders where superscript is not possible.

### Asterisk (*)
All structure/function claims. Always paired with the FDA disclaimer (see `compliance.md`).

### Dagger (†)
Promotions that require terms and conditions.
- Example: `25% Off Your First Order†`

### Double dagger (‡)
Secondary T&C when a dagger is already in use.
- Example: `Refer to Earn $25‡`

## Capitalization (casing)

Use AP style for titles and headers. Capitalize main words including both parts of hyphenated words. Lowercase "little" words (three letters and shorter). To-be verbs (Is, Are) are capitalized in headlines.

### By channel

| Channel | Nav | Banner | Headline | Body | CTA |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Web | Title Case | Title Case | Sentence case with punctuation | Sentence case | Title Case, ≤4 words |
| Email | — | Title Case | Title Case | Sentence case | Title Case |
| Ads | — | — | Title Case | Sentence case | Title Case |
| Social (on graphic) | — | — | Title Case for fragments; Sentence for full sentences | Sentence case | — |
| Social (captions) | — | — | — | Sentence case | — |
| TV (supers) | — | — | Sentence case | — | — |

### CTA words that should stay lowercase (unless first or last)
`a, an, the, and, but, for, or, nor, at, by, for, in, of, off, on, out, to, up, to`

### Headline examples
- AP: "Trust Your Gut-Brain Axis" (Gut-Brain is hyphenated, both parts capped)
- Not AP: "News From the Seed-verse" (missed lowercase "From" and "the"; or hyphenated capitalization)
- Not AP: "Can probiotics boost vaginal health?" (sentence case used where title case is required)

## Numbers

- **Spell out** zero to nine.
- **Use numerals** for 10 and above.
- **Never** start a sentence with a number.
- **Always use numerals** in headlines and titles.

### Always use numerals regardless of size
Time (3 days, 6 months), dosage (2 capsules), supply duration (3-month supply), ages, percentages, dates, times (except noon/midnight), money, measurements, addresses, decimals, fractions.

---

## Time and dates

- Time zone abbreviations: PT, MT, CT, ET. Always include the zone.
- Lowercase with periods: `8 a.m.`, `3 p.m.`
- Single space between numeral and abbreviation.
- Drop `:00` for whole hours.
- Use "to" for ranges: `8 a.m. to 2 p.m.` (not `8 a.m.–2 p.m.`).
- Avoid "12 a.m." and "12 p.m." Use **noon** and **midnight**.

### Per year / month / day
Format with spaces around the slash, full spelling, lowercase.
- `$49.99 / month`
- `3 capsules / day`

## Emojis

Use sparingly. Only where they add meaning that copy alone cannot carry.

### Allowed surfaces
Social, internal comms, occasional email subject lines.

### Never
Product UI, PDPs, packaging, headlines, Cultured articles.

### Rule of thumb
Before dropping an emoji, ask: would a sharper rewrite do the job? If yes, cut the emoji.

### Punctuation order
Punctuation first, space, then emoji.
- Do: `So unlike Vegas, what happens in your vagus doesn't stay there. 🤭`
- Exception (lists): emoji before the comma.
  - `prioritize movement 🏃‍♀️, a fiber-rich diet 🥦, and sleep 💤.`

### Allowed emoji vocabulary
Nature, health, scientific, food themes only: 🌱🌲🌳🥗🥬💚🦠🪷🍃🥑🍄🥕🍁🐚🐝🪲🪰💧💤🌎🧠🤓😉🤯🚀🔍🔬🧪💩🔗🧭⌛⚖️🏃🧘💪🌡️🧬🩺🩻🚽🔭🌕🌓☀️

### Forbidden emoji
Inflammatory, political, religious, suggestive: 😡🖕🙏👊🥊🎉💰💸🤑🍆🔫🎄📿🧨💦👑👘⚔️🪢🔮🤡💀👿😈❤️‍🩹🤕🧕🇺🇲

## Contractions

Use where warmth matters. Skip where precision matters.

### Use
Meta captions, Instagram captions, email subject lines, TV and video VO.

### Avoid
Packaging, PDPs, usage directions, headlines, Cultured articles.

---

## Abbreviations

Use periods.
- `Ph.D.` not `PhD`
- `U.S.` not `US`

---

## Plus signs and ampersands

Plus signs appear in product names and brand phrases. Default to "and" everywhere else.

### Always use `+`
- `AM-02™ Energy + Focus`
- `PM-02™ Sleep + Restore`
- `For You + Your Microbiome`
- `Probiotic + Prebiotic` (when appearing in a headline)

### Use `+` only when character count is fixed and limited
SMS (160 / 70 with special chars), mobile PDP descriptions, PLP product descriptions, Amazon FAQs (200 chars), banners (40 chars), video supers.

### Never use `&` (ampersand)
Always prefer `and` or `+` per the rules above.

### CTAs
Always use `and`, never `+` or `&`.

## Italics and capitalization for species and strains

Bacteria strains need proper italicization and capitalization.

- **Genus + species:** italicized. Genus capitalized. Species never capitalized.
- **Strain identifier:** not italicized. Typically all caps, with exceptions.

Examples:
- *Bifidobacterium breve* SD-BR3-IT
- *Bifidobacterium longum* HRVD90b-US (mixed case strain is fine when the strain uses it)
- *Ophiocordyceps unilateralis* (not all bacteria have strains)

## Lists and bullets

### Casing
- **Short phrases:** Title Case.
- **Full sentences:** Sentence case.

### Punctuation
- **Full sentences:** end each bullet with a period.
- **Fragments:** no terminal punctuation.

### Colons and bullets
- Use a colon to introduce a list only if the intro is a full sentence.
- Don't stack a colon and a bullet. Pick one.
- Reserve `•` for visual emphasis in packaging, not body copy.
